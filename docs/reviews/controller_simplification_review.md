- **Date:** 2026-07-11, branch `claude/review-workflows-controller-leica-yd625w` (commit `e1b2809`).
- **Focus:** bloat, overfitting, overengineering, dead-code fossils, doc drift, readability for the repo's biologist audience, and maintainability — after many AI-assisted hardening rounds.
- **Verification:** the controller suite passes on this tree (35 passed in 0.06 s). Every liveness claim below was checked by repo-wide grep, the behavioral claims (including the private-attribute leak) were reproduced live against the mock driver, and the registry/Session lifecycle was probed with hostile inputs via throwaway scripts (results in section 4a; scripts not committed).
- **Relation to the earlier review:** `docs/reviews/zmart_controller_review.md` (2026-07-03) covered this package finding-by-finding. ## 1. Overview and verdict

The controller is the vendor-agnostic waist of ZMART: a driver registers a table of plain functions (an "ops table") under a connection dict (`registry.py`), `set_instrument` opens a `Session` that forwards each call to the matching driver function (`layer.py`), and `__init__.py` adds a module-level surface so a notebook can write `zmart_controller.set_xyz(...)` without holding a session object. The controller deliberately owns almost nothing: no caching, no coordinate math, no inspection of driver return values.

It is small: 433 lines of source (`__init__.py` 95, `layer.py` 221, `registry.py` 117), 618 lines of tests plus the reference mock, a 251-line README, and two short notebooks. Roughly half of `layer.py` is docstring, which is intentional per `CLAUDE.md`.

**Verdict: healthy, and — unusually for a codebase with this review history — largely free of AI slop.** The hardening rounds did not leave defensive layers or speculative generality here: there are no commented-out blocks, no compatibility shims for vanished callers, no redundant validation wrappers, and the abstraction is proven by two real adapters plus the mock plugging in without controller changes. **Maintainability is high**: one person could confidently change any of the three source modules in six months; each behavior is defined in exactly one place in code (the docs are another matter — see F6/F7). The findings below total perhaps 40–60 removable lines plus a handful of clarity and drift fixes, not surgery. If bloat is the concern, the mass is elsewhere: the Leica driver internals, the workflow widget files, `workflows/.../workflow/retired/` (~8,000 lines of superseded code still on the tree), and the 30-odd accumulated files in `docs/reviews/` are all larger cleanup targets than anything in this package.

## 2. Wiring: workflows → Session → registry → adapters

**Verdict: fully connected, with one soft seam at the acquire-record level (F2) and no missing or misnamed ops.**

- **Registry → adapters.** Both real adapters register complete 13-entry ops tables at import time: Leica at `zmart_adapter/zmart_adapter.py:1223-1248`, mesoSPIM at `mesospim_zmart_adapter.py:600-636`; the mock at `tests/mock_driver.py:287-313`. Every name in `registry.OPS` (`registry.py:39-52`) resolves to a real module-level function in all three, verified against the `def` lines, and `register()` enforces completeness at import (`registry.py:83-85`).
- **Session → adapters, signature by signature.** Every calling convention the controller uses matches both adapters exactly: handle-first everywhere; keyword-only `with_actuators` on `get_xyz`/`set_xyz` (`layer.py:126,137` vs Leica `zmart_adapter.py:532,569-571` and mesoSPIM `mesospim_zmart_adapter.py:313,324-331`); keyword-only `acquisition_type`/`position_label`/`options` on `acquire` (`layer.py:166-171` vs Leica `zmart_adapter.py:811-816`, mesoSPIM `mesospim_zmart_adapter.py:462-467`). The only asymmetry is harmless: Leica's `acquire` defaults `acquisition_type="scan"` and `position_label=None` while the controller signature makes both mandatory — the defaults are unreachable through the controller.
- **Workflows → Session.** Every Session method the live workflow calls exists: `set_origin` (webapp `_flow.py:216`), `get_state`/`set_state` (`_flow.py:223,239,262`; `_capture_run.py:89`), `get_xyz` (`_focus_run.py:41`), `set_xyz` (`_focus_run.py:51`, `_capture_run`), `run_procedure` (`_focus_run.py:54`), `acquire` (`_capture_run`), `get_info` (`_flow.py:203,263`), `disconnect` and the `closed` property (`_run_status.py:40-42`, duck-typed). The workflow's `_simulation.py` mirrors the same surface method-for-method, which keeps the offline path honest.
- **Live vs. speculative surface.** Three Session methods have no automated caller: `get_actuators`, `get_procedures`, and `get_acquisition_options` are exercised only by the controller's own tests, the README, and the example notebooks (`example_leica_experiment.ipynb` cell 8 calls `get_acquisition_options` interactively). They are not orphans to delete — each is the "discover" half of a discover-then-apply pair whose "apply" half is live (`with_actuators` is used in both example notebooks; `run_procedure` and `acquire(options=...)` are used by the workflow) — but the maintainer should know the automated flow never calls them; they exist for the human at the notebook.
- **No orphan adapter surface.** Neither adapter exposes ops the controller cannot reach; extra public names on the Leica adapter package (`CONNECTION`, `ZmartHandle`, `register`) are the documented test/registration seams (`zmart_adapter/__init__.py:7-10`).

## 3. Findings, by value

### F1 — SAFE where noted / JUDGMENT overall — the acquire record has no contract, and the three drivers have drifted on the one key the workflow reads

This is the highest-value finding, and it is a drift/overfit problem rather than bloat. The live workflow consumes the saved-image paths from the acquire record via `record.get("images")` (`workflows/.../workflow/_output.py:120,153`, `_hijack.py:366`; the simulation supplies it, `_simulation.py:215`). The Leica adapter returns `"images"` (`zmart_adapter.py:918`). But mesoSPIM returns `"image_files"` (`mesospim_zmart_adapter.py:545`) and the mock returns only `"filename"` (`tests/mock_driver.py:208`) — so the image-handling steps silently no-op for both (`.get("images")` returns `None`). The controller's "opaque dict the driver owns" stance means nothing pins this: the contract lives as implicit convention, and it has already broken. The README even documents the Leica shape as if universal ("The returned record's `images` list contains the full saved data filenames", `README.md:149-150`) directly under a mock example that produces no such key. **Recommendation:** pick one key (`"images"` — it is what the workflow already reads), state it in the README's driver-contract section as required-when-files-are-saved, align the mock (SAFE, ~3 lines) and mesoSPIM (rename, JUDGMENT since external mesoSPIM users may read `image_files`). Maintainability-wise this is the package's one genuine "behavior defined in N places" problem.

### F2 — SAFE — Three test overfits in `test_layer.py`

1. **A dict key-order assertion.** `tests/test_layer.py:102` pins `list(state) == ["changeable", "observed"]` ("changeable first"). Key order carries no meaning anywhere; this pins an accident of the mock and punishes an innocent refactor. Relax to membership. (1 line.)
2. **Two tests misfiled inside `TestDisconnect`.** `tests/test_layer.py:155-165` — `test_actuator_selection_does_not_persist` and `test_invalid_acquire_option_rejected` belong in `TestFrame` and `TestAcquire`. Pure move; the current placement makes a reader hunt for disconnect relevance that does not exist. (ZC-15, still open.)
3. **A controller test pinning a promise the controller does not make.** `tests/test_layer.py:150-153` (`test_ops_after_disconnect_raise`) passes only because the *mock* raises (`tests/mock_driver.py:92-95`); `Session` ops never check `self._closed` (`layer.py:187-199` guards double-disconnect only). Either relabel it as a driver-contract test (SAFE), or adopt the earlier review's ZC-03 and add the ~4-line guard in `Session` so the guarantee is central and the Leica adapter's thirteen per-op `_require_open` calls become defense-in-depth (JUDGMENT).

### F3 — SAFE — `resolve()` returns its own argument (ZC-11, still open)

`registry.py:102-117` returns `(ops, connection)` where `connection` is literally the `instrument` argument, unchanged; the sole caller already holds it (`layer.py:218`, verified by grep — nothing else calls `resolve`). The tuple is API surface with zero information, and it actively misleads: a reader reasonably assumes `resolve` merges the stored registration dict in, which it does not. Refactor sketch — before: `ops, connection = resolve(instrument)` / `handle = ops["connect"](connection)`; after: `ops = resolve(instrument)` / `handle = ops["connect"](instrument)`. Saves ~4 lines, one concept, and one wrong inference; no test pins the return shape.

### F4 — SAFE — Review-round fossils in the prose (the package's only AI slop)

The code is clean, but a few docstrings carry residue of past review rounds written for the reviewer, not the biologist:

- `tests/mock_driver.py:234-238`: "(operator decision: the identity gate returns only if the changeable part ever grows beyond low-risk settings)" — a compressed changelog entry inside a mock's docstring. No reader can parse "the identity gate returns"; the sentence before it already says everything needed. Delete the parenthetical.
- `layer.py:58-62` (`closed` docstring): the second half — "It deliberately says nothing about an unexpected transport failure the driver has not observed yet — and, because disconnect marks the session closed before calling the driver's teardown, it stays true even if that teardown then raised." — is hedge-stacking that restates the `disconnect` implementation comment (`layer.py:195`). Keep the first sentence; drop the rest. (~4 lines.)
- Copy-paste symmetry: "Returns whatever the driver reports" appears four times in `layer.py` (75, 89-90, 102, 131) and "the controller caches nothing / forwards live" three times (`layer.py:146-147, 183-184, 212-213`) on top of the module docstring; the mock states its "never sticky" rule three times (`mock_driver.py:30-31, 141-144, 181-183`). Once per file plus the implementing helper is enough. (~10 lines across both files; this trims repetition, not information.)

### F5 — SAFE — Small mechanical cleanups

1. **Dead guard:** `__init__.py:69` — `previous is not new` is always true because `layer.set_instrument` constructs a fresh `Session` every call (`layer.py:218-221`); the clause defends against an impossible case and makes the reader hunt for a scenario that cannot occur. Drop it, keep the well-commented ordering around it.
2. **Fixture bypasses the public API (ZC-13, still open):** `tests/conftest.py:36` resets with `zmart_controller._active = None`, leaking the session undisconnected; `zmart_controller.disconnect()` is public, idempotent, and also closes it. One-line swap.

### F6 — SAFE — README/doc drift (each item verified against current code)

1. **`README.md:149-150`** documents the acquire record's `images` list as general behavior; only the Leica adapter produces it (see F1), and the mock example immediately above produces `filename`. Reword to name it as the cross-driver convention F1 establishes (or, until then, as Leica-specific).
2. **`README.md:15-18`** ("The other vendor adapters are still under construction") understates reality: the mesoSPIM adapter exists, registers on import, and `docs/ZMART.md:68` rates it "demo-validated, near production". One sentence brings the status current.
3. **`registry.py:20-24`** likewise names only the Leica adapter as "the first real one" with no mention of mesoSPIM — same one-line fix.
4. Checked and *not* drifted (for the record): the README's Leica connection dict (`README.md:84-85`) matches the adapter's `CONNECTION` (`zmart_adapter.py:100-118`); the actuator names (`README.md:117-118`) match `_ACTUATORS` (`zmart_adapter.py:120`); the Leica option-menu keys (`README.md:152-156`) match `get_acquisition_options` (`zmart_adapter.py:699-705`); the example notebooks run against the current `changeable`/`observed` contract.

### F7 — JUDGMENT — one contract stated in three prose places

The "ops report failure by raising, `ValueError` for caller mistakes, `RuntimeError` for instrument failures, never encode failure in the dict, keep error text credential-safe" contract is written out in full three times: `layer.py:12-15`, `registry.py:13-18`, and `README.md:231-237`. Each copy is well written, but three copies of one rule is exactly how doc drift starts, and it is the package's main "defined in N places" maintainability cost after F1. Keep the README version (the front door for driver authors) and reduce the two module docstrings to one sentence plus a pointer. (~8 lines, and one future drift mode removed.)

### F8 — JUDGMENT — vendor-specific teaching examples inside the vendor-agnostic layer

`layer.py:122-124` (get_xyz: "the Leica driver's z, for example, reads the same regardless") and the Leica naming-rule specifics at `README.md:152-156` embed concrete Leica behavior in the agnostic surface. This was a deliberate fix for the earlier review's ZC-05 (examples that matched no real driver), and it genuinely helps a learner — but nothing tests these lines, so they are standing drift liabilities (F6 shows this class of rot is real). Middle path: keep the one worked Leica connection dict in the README (load-bearing), replace scattered menu/naming specifics with a pointer to the driver README. ~8 lines, traded against some hand-holding; the maintainer should weigh the CLAUDE.md audience rule.

### F9 — JUDGMENT — the one structural refactor with leverage: make the package installable (ZC-14, still open)

`pyproject.toml` still has no `[project]` table (lint config only), so three separate path hacks compensate: `tests/conftest.py:10-17`, `example_experiment.ipynb` cell 2, `example_leica_experiment.ipynb` cell 1. Worse, the same file is importable under two names — the controller's conftest imports top-level `mock_driver`, while workflow tests import `zmart_controller.tests.mock_driver` (`workflows/.../tests/test_capture_run.py:11`, working only because `tests/` is an implicit namespace subpackage). Two module objects for one file means duplicate registrations ("already registered; overwriting" warnings, last-wins by design) and two distinct `MockHandle` classes in one pytest run. Refactor sketch — before: three sys.path shims plus dual import identity; after: a ~10-line `[project]` table, `pip install -e .`, conftest shrinks to the register call, the notebooks lose their setup cells, one import spelling everywhere (which also retires the Leica notebook's second spelling, ZC-17). More than mechanical (it changes how everyone runs the code), but it deletes a *category* of workaround rather than lines.

## 4. Things that look like real bugs (flagged, not fixed)

1. **Module `__getattr__` leaks the session's private internals (ZC-06, still open).** `__init__.py:87-95` delegates any attribute the active session has, with no underscore guard on the delegating branch — verified live: with a session active, `zmart_controller._ops` returns the ops dict and `zmart_controller._handle` the driver handle. This contradicts "The only public attribute is `context`" (`layer.py:32`). One-line fix (refuse `name.startswith("_")` before delegating); grep confirms nothing depends on the leak.
2. **The workflow's image handling silently no-ops on two of three drivers** — the F1 key drift (`images` / `image_files` / `filename`). On mesoSPIM, `_output.py`'s record-relocation and `_hijack.py`'s streaming both find no `"images"` and skip; no error is raised. Flagged here because the failure mode is silent.
3. **The mock's `run_procedure` record shape drifted from both real adapters.** Mock: `"ran": dict(procedure)` (`tests/mock_driver.py:268-269`, pinned by `test_layer.py:128`); mesoSPIM: `"ran": <name string>` (`mesospim_zmart_adapter.py:429,434`); Leica is internally inconsistent — dict at `zmart_adapter.py:1049`, string at `zmart_adapter.py:1096`. No live consumer reads `"ran"` (`_focus_run.py:56` reads only `frame_z_um`/`focus_um`), so this is latent — but the "faithful reference" mock teaches a shape no real driver uses consistently. One decision, three small edits.
4. **`get_instruments()` copies are shallow** (`registry.py:99`): a nested dict inside a connection dict would be shared with the registry. All three registered connections are flat today (verified), so this is a docstring caveat ("keep connection dicts flat"), not a defect.

## 4a. Adversarial probe results

The registry and the module-level lifecycle were exercised with hostile inputs (malformed ops tables, drivers raising at connect, mid-call, and teardown, double disconnects, broken identity values). **The lifecycle passed everything**, which strongly supports keeping the orderings in section 5 item 4: a `connect` that raises mid-swap leaves the previous session alive and the module still delegating to it; an op raising mid-call leaves the session usable; a teardown raising during module `disconnect` still leaves the module cleanly inactive; double disconnect and reconnect are no-drama. Two registration-time gaps produce confusing failures:

1. **A poisoned identity value breaks instrument discovery for everyone.** `register()` accepts `None` (or any non-string) as an identity value — plausible, since the shipped connection templates use `None` placeholders for non-identity keys (`zmart_adapter.py:106-107`) and a copied template with `"microscope": None` registers without complaint. The failure then surfaces far away: `get_instruments()` raises `TypeError: '<' not supported between instances of 'NoneType' and 'str'` from `sorted(REGISTRY.items())` (`registry.py:99`) for *every* caller, with no hint which registration is at fault. Repro: `register({"vendor": None, "microscope": "s", "api": "a"}, ops=<full table>)` then `get_instruments()`. One defensive line in `_identity` (require `str` values, name the offending key) is justified here — it converts a global, opaque break into a local, immediate one.
2. **Non-callable ops register fine and fail later with a bare `TypeError` (ZC-12, still open).** `register(conn, ops={name: None for name in OPS})` succeeds; `set_instrument` then dies with `TypeError: 'NoneType' object is not callable` — no driver name, no op name. The completeness check at `registry.py:83-85` already exists; extending it to `not callable(ops.get(name))` costs zero new structure. This one bites adapter authors, not operators, so it is lower value than item 1.

Also probed and judged acceptable as-is: an unhashable identity value fails immediately at `register()` with `TypeError: unhashable type` (near the mistake — fine); registering with both a broken identity and missing ops reports the identity error first (the ops error message is built with `_identity(connection)`, `registry.py:85`) — mildly surprising but harmless, and the message stays credential-safe.

## 5. Overengineered-looking things that should be KEPT

1. **Twelve hand-written forwarding methods on `Session`** (`layer.py:67-199`) instead of `__getattr__` magic over the ops dict. ~140 lines buy real signatures, per-method docstrings, and tab completion — the right trade for a notebook-first surface aimed at learners. Likewise the three explicit 13-line ops-table literals in the drivers: a `{name: globals()[name] for name in OPS}` one-liner would save lines and cost the exact readability this repo optimizes for. Considered and rejected.
2. **Copy discipline in the registry** (`registry.py:89,99`) with both directions tested (`test_registry.py:44-55`): the README promises "edit the dict before connecting", and the copies are what make that safe.
3. **Credential-safe error messages** (`_identity`, `registry.py:62-70`) and the test asserting `"hunter2"` never appears (`test_registry.py:31-36`). Threat-aware without ceremony.
4. **The teardown orderings with their comments and tests**: closed-before-teardown (`layer.py:193-199`), resolve-new-before-disconnect-old and track-before-teardown (`__init__.py:63-71`), failing-teardown edge tested (`test_layer.py:186-195`). On hardware these prevent retried teardowns and leaked sessions; every line earns its place.
5. **`Session.closed`** (`layer.py:53-63`): looks unused inside the package, but the workflow's run-status duck-types it (`_run_status.py:40-42`) and the simulation mirrors it (`_simulation.py:124`). Consumed; keep (trim its docstring per F4).
6. **The all-twelve-ops completeness check** (`registry.py:83-85`): strict-looking, but all three drivers fill the table without friction, and fail-fast at registration beats a `KeyError` mid-experiment.
7. **`test_layer.py` exercising mock behavior through the Session** (`test_layer.py:46-128`): read narrowly, ~70 lines pin a test fixture; read as intended, they are the executable driver contract, and maintainer decision #3 (`docs/reviews/MAINTAINER_DECISIONS.md`) explicitly wants the seam tested through the controller. Keep, and add a two-line comment saying so, so the next reviewer does not flag them again.
8. **The discovery methods with no automated caller** (`get_actuators`, `get_procedures`, `get_acquisition_options` — see Wiring): they are the "discover" half of live discover-then-apply pairs and exist for the human at the notebook. Cheap, coherent, keep.
9. **The verbose docstrings generally.** Half of `layer.py` is prose and `CLAUDE.md` says that is the product. Trim the repetition and the review fossils (F4), never the explanation.
